4.2.823 Signal

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Signal is a type of Managed_design_object. A Signal in this part of ISO 10303 is an abstraction of signal as categorized according to an ISO or IEC standard. Signal in this part of ISO 10303 is specifically included for the purpose of exchanging information related to functional abstractions of products as commonly specified on data sheets. There is no predefined mechanism in this part of ISO 10303 that defines standard physical arrangement of instrumentation and test articles to support test processes. This part of ISO 10303 provides a standard mechanism to associate sets of data that characterize the behaviour of a product with the functional hierarchical model and physical geometric model of that product.

NOTES:

  1. A Signal is a physical phenomenon that may be characterized by means of appropriate instrumentation or simulated by a computer system for the purpose of evaluating system behaviour.
  2. IEC Multi-Lingual Dictionary Volume 2, 1992: signal (174) a physical phenomenon, one or more of whose characteristics may vary to represent information.

EXAMPLE: The physical phenomenon may be, for instance, an electromagnetic wave, or acoustic wave and the characteristic may be an electric field, a voltage, or a sound pressure.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • signal_category

4.2.823.1 signal_category

The signal_category specifies the External_definition for the category for this Signal.

EXAMPLE: Examples of signal_category include an electromagnetic wave, or acoustic wave.
