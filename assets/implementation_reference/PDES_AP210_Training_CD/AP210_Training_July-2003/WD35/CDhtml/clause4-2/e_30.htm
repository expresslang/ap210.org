4.2.30 Assembled_with_bonding

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
An Assembled_with_bonding is a type of Assembly_joint that establishes the joint by bonding. Bonding requires use of material that may change state during the bonding process to form a permanent or semi-permanent joint. In this context, a permanent or semi-permanent joint means that repair of the affected part of the assembly would require destruction and replacement of the bond material.

NOTE: The Assembled_with_bonding object relates two assembly features that are bonded and the Assembly_bond_definition that specifies the category of the bond, its shape, its material, and its name.

EXAMPLE: Figure awb illustrates the concept of the joint category described herein and the roles of the related application objects.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • bond_assembly_position
  • default_bond_definition

4.2.30.1 bond_assembly_position

The bond_assembly_position is an inverse relationship that specifies that the Assembled_with_bonding shall be referenced by no more than one Bond_assembly_position as its bond. The bond_assembly_position need not be specified for a particular Assembled_with_bonding. The position is provided to identify shape placement for analysis.

4.2.30.2 default_bond_definition

The default_bond_definition specifies the Assembly_bond_definition used by the Assembled_with_bonding process to form a join.

EXAMPLE: A type of bond material is solder. A type of component feature is a component terminal.

Formal constraints:

WR1: The members of Part_feature utilized by Assembled_with_bonding that are inherited from Assembly_joint shall be the same members of Part_feature specified inclusively by Assembly_bond_definition bonded_feature_1 and bonded_feature_2.
