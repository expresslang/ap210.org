4.2.92 Centre_plane

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Centre_plane is a type of Centre_of_symmetry that is planar in form. The Shape_element derived from the Centre_plane is associated with the Centre_plane by the Shape_element_deriving_relationship that are specified as the deriving_relationships.

NOTES:

  1. The attribute deriving_relationships is declared in the Derived_shape_element entity of which this entity is a subtype.
  2. The precise interpretation of a Centre_plane is context dependent. That is, the distinction is not made between the centre plane of a feature and a derived median plane of a feature as defined in 1.3 of ASME Y14.5M.

EXAMPLE: A Position_tolerance and a Straightness_tolerance may be applied to a Centre_plane that is derived from a Composite_unit_shape_element that consists of two opposing surfaces of a notch (or tab).

Informal constraints:

IP1: The form of the Shape_elements from which the Centre_plane is derived shall be consistent with the form of shape elements from which a centre plane can be derived.
