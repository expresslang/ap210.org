4.2.51 Assembly_module_usage_view

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
An Assembly_module_usage_view is a type of Physical_unit_usage_view. An Assembly_module_usage_view may be a Pca_usage_view. An Assembly_module_usage_view provides additional domain knowledge for the design organization.

NOTES:

  1. The Assembly_module_usage_view supports intellectual property protection. A design organization can control the amount of data they provide to a licensee or purchaser by including only a specifically tailored set of data in a usage view.
  2. Shape information is provided by Physical_unit_3d_shape or by Physical_unit_planar_shape.

EXAMPLES:

  1. This data can include the information currently included in an interface control drawing (ICD).
  2. Figure amuv illustrates that the information in an Assembly_module_usage_view can be different from the information in a design view. The information in this illustration is sufficient to analyse airflow characteristics and interference characteristics, as well as information related to the connectors in the design.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • access_mechanisms
  • implemented_function
  • maximum_negative_component_height
  • maximum_positive_component_height

4.2.51.1 access_mechanisms

The access_mechanisms is an inverse relationship that specifies that the existence of the Assembly_module_usage_view is dependent on the existence of the Assembly_module_terminal that specifies the Assembly_module_usage_view as its associated_definition. There shall be two or more Assembly_module_terminals for an Assembly_module_usage_view.

4.2.51.2 implemented_function

The implemented_function specifies the role of the Functional_unit_usage_view for the Assembly_module_usage_view.

4.2.51.3 maximum_negative_component_height

The maximum_negative_component_height specifies the largest Datum_based_length_measure in the negative direction from the Datum for the Assembly_module_usage_view. The maximum_negative_component_height need not be specified for a particular Assembly_module_usage_view.

EXAMPLE: An example of the maximum_negative_component_height attribute is for a printed circuit assembly with through-hole components, where the maximum lead protrusion dimension is given. That dimension is the maximum_negative_component_height. Figure amuv illustrates this example. Another example of the maximum_negative_component_height attribute is for a printed circuit assembly with surface mounted components on both sides. The maximum_negative_component_height attribute is for the distance between the established datum and the maximum material condition of the vertical dimension of the highest surface mount component on the secondary side of the printed circuit assembly.

4.2.51.4 maximum_positive_component_height

The maximum_positive_component_height specifies the largest Datum_based_length_measure in the positive direction from the Datum for the Assembly_module_usage_view. The maximum_positive_component_height need not be specified for a particular Assembly_module_usage_view.

EXAMPLE: An example of the maximum_positive_component_height attribute is for a printed circuit assembly where the maximum component height in the positive direction relative to a datum is given. This dimension pertains to the fit of the printed circuit assembly in its next assembly. That dimension is the maximum_positive_component_height. Figure amuv illustrates this example.

Formal constraints:

WR1: The direction from the Datum for the maximum_negative_component_height shall be negative in the Z axis direction.

WR2: The direction from the Datum for the maximum_positive_component_height shall be positive in the Z axis direction.

WR3: The function implemented shall be the function whose terminals are mapped to the terminals of this assembly module usage view.

Informal constraints:

IP1: The Datum orientation vector direction shall be considered to be positive when it is parallel to the normal of the top side of the interconnect substrate instance.

ENTITY assembly_module_usage_view
  SUPERTYPE OF (pca_usage_view)
  SUBTYPE OF (physical_unit_usage_view);
    implemented_function              : functional_unit_usage_view;
    maximum_negative_component_height : OPTIONAL datum_based_length_measure;
    maximum_positive_component_height : OPTIONAL datum_based_length_measure;
INVERSE
    access_mechanisms : SET [2:?] OF assembly_module_terminal FOR 
                        associated_definition;
END_ENTITY; -- assembly_module_usage_view
