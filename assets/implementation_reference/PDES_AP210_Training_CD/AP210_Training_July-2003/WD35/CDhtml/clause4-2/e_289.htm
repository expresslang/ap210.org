4.2.289 Dielectric_material_passage

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Dielectric_material_passage is a type of Inter_stratum_feature that contains dielectric material. The extent of the Dielectric_material_passage is between the boundary defined by the shape_definition of this Dielectric_material_passage and the shape of the Cutout that is to be filled with dielectric material during manufacture.

EXAMPLE: Figure dmp illustrates an exploded, stylized, detail of a Dielectric_material_passage as it appears in two sequential stages of the fabrication process.

EXAMPLE: A Dielectric_material_passage is used, for example, in printed circuit boards with metal core Stratum for thermal conductivity, that have oversized passages created through the metal Stratum before bonding. These oversized passages in the metal Stratum are filled with dielectric material during the bonding process for later machining to suit the eventual purpose for the passage at that location. These smaller passages are often plated for electrical continuity among Design_layer_stratums on either side of the metal core, but there must be electrical isolation between the plating in the passage and the metal thermal conductive Stratum. The dielectric fill material provides that isolation.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • feature_material
  • precedent_passage

4.2.289.1 feature_material

The feature_material specifies either the Ee_material or the Ee_requirement_occurrence that defines the dielectric material contained in the Dielectric_material_passage. The feature_material need not be specified for a particular Dielectric_material_passage.

4.2.289.2 precedent_passage

The precedent_passage specifies the Cutout that defines the outer boundary of the Dielectric_material_passage before it is filled with the specified non-conductive Ee_material.

Formal constraints:

WR1: The same material shall exist throughout the extent of the passage.
