4.2.698 Positional_boundary

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Positional_boundary is a type of Non_feature_shape_element that is a theoretical boundary that is defined by a Boundary_based_position_tolerance. The theoretical boundary is a boundary that cannot be intruded upon by the toleranced Physical_feature_or_part_template if the Physical_feature_or_part_template is an internal Physical_feature_or_part_template (e.g., a hole) and the Boundary_based_position_tolerance is applied on a "maximum material principle" basis, or if the toleranced Physical_feature_or_part_template is an external Physical_feature_or_part_template (e.g., a tab) and the Boundary_based_position_tolerance is applied on a "least material requirement" basis. Additionally, the theoretical boundary is a boundary that cannot be escaped by the toleranced Physical_feature_or_part_template if the Physical_feature_or_part_template is an internal Physical_feature_or_part_template and the Boundary_based_position_tolerance is applied on a "least material requirement" basis, or if the toleranced Physical_feature_or_part_template is an external Physical_feature_or_part_template and the Boundary_based_position_tolerance is applied on a "maximum material principle" basis. A Positional_boundary is either a Profile_related_positional_boundary or a Dimension_related_positional_boundary.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • boundary_definition

4.2.698.1 boundary_definition

The boundary_definition is an inverse relationship that specifies that the existence of the Positional_boundary is dependent on the existence of the Positional_boundary_definition that specifies the Positional_boundary as its defined_positional_boundary.
