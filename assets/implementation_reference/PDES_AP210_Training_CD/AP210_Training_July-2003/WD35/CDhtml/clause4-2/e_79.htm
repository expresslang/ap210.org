4.2.80 Bounding_curve

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Bounding_curve is a type of Curve . A Bounding_curve is provided in this part of ISO 10303 to describe the left side or the right side of a Conductive_interconnect_element_with_user_defined_single_transition on an interconnect substrate.

EXAMPLE: Figure bc1 illustrates a detail of a layout drawing that shows a transition on a trace. Figure bc2 illustrate several alternative functions (only one of which is used in this example). A selected function describes the mapping from a constant to the shape of the top side of the trace segment identified as "l" in subfigure "C" in figure bc1. The segment "l" is supported in this part of ISO 10303 through a member of Conductive_interconnect_element_with_user_defined_single_transition.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • basis_curve
  • mapping_function

4.2.80.1 basis_curve

The basis_curve specifies the role of the Trimmed_line for the Bounding_curve. The basis_curve founds the Bounding_curve in the geometric context of the design.

4.2.80.2 mapping_function

The mapping_function specifies the role of the External_definition for the Bounding_curve. The mapping_function maps the Trimmed_line onto the resultant curve which is the Bounding_curve.

NOTE: This mechanism ensures that the geometry may be exchanged using this part of ISO 103030 even though the mapping function is an externally defined concept.

Formal constraints:

WR1: A Bounding_curve shall not specify itself through the basis_curve, either directly or indirectly.

WR2: The mapping_function shall have the property of being a one to one map.
