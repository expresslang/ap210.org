4.2.59 Bare_die

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Bare_die is a type of a Physical_unit_usage_view. A Bare_die is used to describe the physical shape and function of a semiconductor device.

NOTES:

  1. In the PCA domain an occurrence of a Bare_die may be installed into an occurrence of a Package to realize a Packaged_part, an occurrence of which is then mounted in the assembly. In this role of performing a function, a Bare_die has terminals.
  2. In the PCB domain an occurrence of a Bare_die may be installed into the design of an Interconnect_module to realize an embedded component. In this role, the external surfaces of the Bare_die will be important as they form part of the location information for the occurrence.
  3. A Bare_die implements electrical functionality. It is not necessary to exchange Bare_die information for parts such as molded or ceramic resistors where there is not a separately identifiable and common industry understanding of a die.
  4. In the PCA domain an occurrence of a Bare_die may be mounted directly onto the occurrence of a usage view of a PCB.
  5. The existence of oxide or other thin contaminate protection is considered normal for a Bare_die.

EXAMPLE: A semiconductor die is a familiar example used in packaged components on PCAs.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • access_mechanisms
  • implemented_function
  • least_material_condition_centroid_location
  • maximum_height_above_die_seating_plane
  • maximum_material_condition_centroid_location
  • die_seating_plane
  • unit_technology

4.2.59.1 access_mechanisms

The access_mechanisms is an inverse relationship that specifies that the existence of the Bare_die is dependent on the existence of the Minimally_defined_bare_die_terminal that specifies the Bare_die as its associated_definition. There shall be two or more Minimally_defined_bare_die_terminal for a Bare_die.

4.2.59.2 implemented_function

The implemented_function specifies the role of the Functional_unit_usage_view for the Bare_die.

4.2.59.3 least_material_condition_centroid_location

The least_material_condition_centroid_location specifies one role of the Cartesian_point for the Bare_die. The least_material_condition_centroid_location is the center of mass for a bare die in the minimum material state. The least_material_condition_centroid_location need not be specified for a particular Bare_die.

4.2.59.4 maximum_height_above_die_seating_plane

The maximum_height_above_die_seating_plane specifies the role of the Length_data_element for the Bare_die. The maximum_height_above_die_seating_plane is the largest extent of the Bare_die above the die_seating_plane. The maximum_height_above_die_seating_plane need not be specified for a particular Bare_die.

4.2.59.5 maximum_material_condition_centroid_location

The maximum_material_condition_centroid_location specifies one role of the Cartesian_point for the Bare_die. The maximum_material_condition_centroid_location is the center of mass for a bare die in the state that contains the most material. The maximum_material_condition_centroid_location need not be specified for a particular Bare_die.

4.2.59.6 die_seating_plane

The die_seating_plane is an inverse relationship that specifies that the existence of the Bare_die is dependent on the existence of the Seating_plane that is also a standard orientation reference for assembly purposes for the Bare_die. The seating_plane is simulating a mounting surface.

4.2.59.7 unit_technology

The unit_technology specifies the role of the Technology for the Bare_die. This part of ISO 10303 does not predefined unit_technology classification.

EXAMPLE: Examples include a classification for process and material such as CMOS, BICMOS, ECL, etc.

NOTES:

  1. Additional categorization is supported with this part of ISO 10303 by using Ee_product_category and related application objects.
  2. The Bare_die is a monolithic (silicon, germanium, gallium arsenide, etc.) component before encapsulation in a package. It has generally been passivated (e.g., with an oxide layer) to protect it from contamination. Packaging is not required in order for a Bare_die description to be exchanged in conformance to this standard.

Formal constraints:

WR1: The function implemented shall be the function whose terminals are mapped to the terminals of the bare die.

ENTITY bare_die
  SUBTYPE OF (physical_unit_usage_view);
    least_material_condition_centroid_location   : OPTIONAL cartesian_point;
    maximum_height_above_seating_plane           : OPTIONAL length_data_element;
    maximum_material_condition_centroid_location : OPTIONAL cartesian_point;
    SELF\physical_unit.unit_technology           : technology;
    implemented_function                         : functional_unit_usage_view;
  INVERSE
    access_mechanisms : SET [2:?] OF minimally_defined_bare_die_terminal FOR 
                        associated_definition;
    die_seating_plane                            : non_feature_shape_element FOR
                        scope;
  UNIQUE
    ur1 : implemented_function, unit_technology;
WHERE
 WR1: SIZEOF(QUERY ( pf <* USEDIN (SELF,
   'AP210_ARM.PART_FEATURE.ASSOCIATED_DEFINITION') |
    ('AP210_ARM.' +
     'BARE_DIE_TOP_SURFACE' IN TYPEOF (pf))))
     <= 1;
 WR2: SIZEOF(QUERY ( pf <* USEDIN (SELF,
   'AP210_ARM.PART_FEATURE.ASSOCIATED_DEFINITION') |
    ('AP210_ARM.' +
     'BARE_DIE_BOTTOM_SURFACE' IN TYPEOF (pf))))
     <= 1;
 WR3: SIZEOF(QUERY ( pf <* USEDIN (SELF,
   'AP210_ARM.PART_FEATURE.ASSOCIATED_DEFINITION') |
    ('AP210_ARM.' +
     'BARE_DIE_EDGE_SURFACE' IN TYPEOF (pf))))
     <= 1;
  WR4:'AP210_ARM.SEATING_PLANE' IN TYPEOF(die_seating_plane);
END_ENTITY; -- bare_die
