4.2.921 Tolerance_zone_definition_with_per_unit_size_specification

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Tolerance_zone_with_per_unit_size_specification is a type of Tolerance_zone_definition in which the extent of the Tolerance_zone is defined. A Tolerance_zone_with_per_unit_size_specification is used to define a Tolerance_zone when the design requirements are such that abrupt variations in the toleranced Shape_element must be limited. In essence, the thickness, or the diameter, of the Tolerance_zone is defined by the Boundary_size_characteristic and the extent is defined by the Tolerance_zone_per_unit_size_specification. Each element of the toleranced Shape_element that has the same extent as the Tolerance_zone must lie within the Tolerance_zone when the Tolerance_zone is positioned coincident with the element.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • per_unit_size_specification

4.2.921.1 per_unit_size_specification

The per_unit_size_specification specifies the Tolerance_zone_per_unit_size_specification that defines the extent of the specified Tolerance_zone for the Tolerance_zone_definition_with_per_unit_size_specification.

EXAMPLE: A Straightness_tolerance may be applied on a per unit basis such that every 100 mm length of a toleranced axis must lie within a Tolerance_zone that is bounded by a cylindrical Tolerance_zone that is 3 mm in diameter.

Formal constraints:

WR1: The Tolerance_zone specified as the defined_tolerance_zone shall be the same instance of Tolerance_zone as specified as the specified_tolerance_zone by the Tolerance_zone_per_unit_size_specification that is specified as the per_unit_size_specification.

NOTE: The attribute defined_tolerance_zone is declared in the Tolerance_zone_definition entity of which this entity is a subtype.

WR2: The Geometric_tolerance that specifies the Tolerance_zone_definition_with_per_unit_size_specification as the tolerance_zone_or_boundary_definition shall be one of Flatness_tolerance, Straightness_tolerance, Angularity_tolerance, Parallelism_tolerance, or Perpendicularity_tolerance.
