4.2.205 Curve_style

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Curve_style is a type of Managed_design_object. A Curve_style is the definition of a set of properties that are of a geometric nature. Curve_style provides parametric data used by a processor to create physical material along a path, where the path is described outside of the style. The Curve_style algorithm is rule based and is formally described in the domain rules. The width_uncertainty represents the uncertainty in the originating CAD system, not a material processing tolerance.

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • corner_style
  • curve_font
  • curve_width
  • end_extension
  • end_style
  • name

4.2.205.1 corner_style

The corner_style specifies either the "extend" or "chord 2 extend" or "round" or "truncate" style for the Curve_style. Figures cs2 and cs3 illustrate the construction options associated with "extend", "chord 2 extend" and "truncate" corners. These illustrations shall be interpreted as describing construction of the outer boundary near the intersection only. At some distance away from the intersection, the basic width attribute governs the shape. Figure cs4 illustrates the construction associated with round corners. Figure cs5 illustrates the construction of the end treatment of the curve. The end treatment is applied individually to each end. Figure cs6 illustrates the completed result for a Curve that has been styled using this Curve_style. Even though the Curve does not show material inside, that is required if the Curve is applied to material.

4.2.205.2 curve_font

The curve_font specifies either the Curve_style_font_and_scaling, or one of Curve_style_font, Externally_defined_curve_font, or Solid_curve for the Curve_style. The curve_font need not be specified.

4.2.205.3 curve_width

The curve_width specifies the role of the Length_data_element for the Curve_style.

4.2.205.4 end_extension

The end_extension specifies how the physical material will terminate on the Layer_connection_point that is the start or end of the centreline curve. The end_extension is applied by a continuation (termination) of the Curve that is being styled along the tangent to the Curve at the terminus of the Curve. If the end_extension is "extend", there will be material added after the Layer_connection_point. If the end_extension is "truncate", there will be no material added after the Layer_connection_point.

NOTE: This Application Object does not describe tolerances associated with fabrication processes. Reference Stratum_technology as an example of tolerance information related to fabrication processes.

4.2.205.5 end_style

The end_style specifies either the "round" or "square" style endsfor the Curve_style. Figures cs2 and cs3 illustrate the construction options associated with square corners. These illustrations shall be interpreted as describing construction of the outer boundary near the end only. At some distance away from the end, the basic width attribute governs the shape. Figure cs5 illustrates the construction of the end treatment of the curve. The end treatment is applied individually to each end. Figure cs6 illustrates the completed result for a Curve that has been styled using this Curve_style. Even though the Curve does not show material inside, that is required if the Curve is applied to material.

4.2.205.6 name

The name specifies the string for the identifier for the Curve_style.

Formal constraints:

UR1: The name shall be unique within a population of Curve_style.

WR1: When the end_extension is "truncate", the Layer_connection_point for the end of interest shall be the terminus of the curve.

WR2: When the end_style is "square", the centreline of the square shall be congruent with the tangent of the centreline curve at the Layer_connection_point for the end of interest.

WR3: When the end_style is "round" and the end_extension is "extend", the Layer_connection_point for the end of interest shall be the center of a circle with radius equal to 0.5 times width.

WR4: When the end_style is "square" and the end_extension is "extend", the Layer_connection_point for the end of interest shall be the center of a square with side equal to the width.

WR5: When the corner_style is "extend", the final shape shall be the result of a union operation and a fill operation. The union operation shall be between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourho od of the Cartesian_point. The fill operation shall maximize the material enclosed while maintaining the following conditions: The fill boundary shall be two lines that are extensions of the right hand boundary of the filled material as it traverses the corner, in the direction of the centreline curve.

WR6: When the corner_style is "chord 2 extend", the final shape shall be the result of an intersection and fill operation between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourhood of the Cartesian_point, where there is a 2 chord approximation to a circular arc applied, which has radius equal to 0.5 times width. The final shape shall be the result of a fill operation after the union operation is performed between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourhood of the Cartesian_point. The circular arc shall be constructed as required by the "round" corner_style rule.

WR7: When the corner_style is "truncate", the final shape shall be the result of an union operation followed by a fill operation. The union operation shall be between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourhood of the Cartesian_point, where there is a bevel end applied. The final shape shall be the result of a fill operation after the union operation is performed between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourhood of the Cartesian_point. The fill operation shall maximize the material enclosed while maintaining the following conditions: The fill boundary line shall be perpendicular to the angle bisector formed between the two Curve segments at the Cartesian_point of interest; the fill boundary line shall intersect the previously described result of the union operation in exactly two places; the fill boundary line shall not contain the Cartesian_point of interest.

WR8: When the corner_style is "round", the final shape shall be the result of a union operation between the styled curve segments approaching the Cartesian_point of interest in the neighbourhood of the Cartesian_point, followed by a fill operation with a circular arc of radius 0.5 times width applied. The circular arc shall be centred on the Cartesian_point.

WR9: When the Curve to which the Curve_style is applied is closed, end_treatment shall be ignored.

ENTITY curve_style
  SUBTYPE OF (managed_design_object);
    name          : STRING;
    corner_style  : extend_or_chord_2_extend_or_truncate_or_round;
    curve_width   : length_data_element;
    curve_font    : OPTIONAL curve_font_or_scaled_curve_font_select;
    end_extension : extend_or_truncate;
    end_style     : square_or_round;
    width_uncertainty : length_data_element;
UNIQUE
  ur1: name;
END_ENTITY; -- curve_style
