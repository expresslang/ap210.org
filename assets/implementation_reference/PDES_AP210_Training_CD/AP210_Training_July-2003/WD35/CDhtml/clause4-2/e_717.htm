4.2.717 Primary_orientation_feature

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Primary_orientation_feature is a type of Part_feature and is a type of Datum_feature. A Primary_orientation_feature participates in providing for the unambiguos orientation of a part on its next assembly.

NOTE: A Primary_orientation_feature is not necessarily also a Primary_reference_terminal.

EXAMPLE: Figure pof8 illustrates two of the three-dimensional geometric relationships between a component and the printed circuit board it is mounted on. A Datum_system is included for reference purposes. The Z direction is parallel to the normal of the top surface of the board. Both the top and bottom surface normal vectors are parallel to the normal vectors of the seating plane for the components.

EXAMPLE: Figure pkgxfrm illustrates two of the two-dimensional geometric relationships between a component and the printed circuit board it is mounted on. A Datum_system is included for reference purposes.

EXAMPLE: Figures pof7 through pof3 illustrates orientation details for microcircuit packages.

EXAMPLE: Figure pof2 illustrates details associated with several variations of an Axial package modification and the associated seating_plane. In all these cases, the seating plane orientation with respect to the Axis_placement is constant, while the Package shape orientation changes due to the modification.

EXAMPLE: Figure pof1 illustrates Primary_reference_terminal assignment and also assignment of Polarity_indication_feature. Knowledge of Polarity_indication_features separate from the orientation feature increases the ability to determine when to use polarity indication symbols on the interconnect substrate for polarized components e.g., polarized capacitors, diodes).
