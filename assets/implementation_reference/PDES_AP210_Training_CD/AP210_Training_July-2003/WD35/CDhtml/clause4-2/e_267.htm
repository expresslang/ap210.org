4.2.267 Design_intent_modification

NOTE: The text description for this Application Object has not been fully updated to agree with the EXPRESS.
A Design_intent_modification is a type of Managed_design_object. A Design_intent_modification may be one of Material_addition_feature, Material_removal_feature, or Material_shape_modification. A Design_intent_modification provides capabilities to archive design intent along with constraint based modifications so that if a constraint is removed the original design intent, or a part thereof, may be restored.

EXAMPLE: This example shows how this part of ISO 10303 may be used to exchange or archive design intent for the case of a power plane where there is penetration of the power plane by other signals. Figure dim1 is the original design intent for Power Plane 1 that is captured in a member of Connected_filled_area(#1). Figure dim2 is the original design intent for Power Plane 2 that is captured in a member of Connected_filled_area (#2). Figure dim3 is the original design intent for Signal 3 that is captured in a member of Conductor(#3). In order to accomplish the embedding of Power Plane 2 into Power Plane 1 and in order to accomplish the embedding of Signal 3 into the result of the previous embedding, a sequence is followed. In order to meet requirements, constraints must be satisfied regarding spacing of conductive areas. These constraints are specified by the Ee_requirement_occurrence. Note that the following method is based on the relative priority of the signal and the two power planes. This example assumes that the conductor has the highest priority for area, followed by Power Plane 2 followed by Power Plane 1 which has the lowest priority. Step 1: Create three "design intent" Stratum_features as above. Note that these features are a permanent part of the design. Step 2: Create a member of Material_removal_stratum_feature_template (see figure dim4) for the spacing requirement to be met between Power Plane 2 and Power Plane 1. This template shape is dependent on the voltage between Power Plane 2 and Power Plane 1. The distance between the outer extent of Power Plane 2 and the outer extent of the template shape is explicitly stated in the electrical_isolation_spacing_requirement between the Power Plane 2 and Power Plane 1. Populate member of Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure to capture the stated relationships and properties. For further reference in this example, this member will be called 1. Step 3: Create a Material_removal_stratum_feature_template (see figure dim5) for the spacing requirement to be met between Signal 3 and Power Plane 1. This template shape is dependent on the voltage between Signal 3 and Power Plane 1. The distance between the outer extent of Signal 3 and the outer extent of the template shape is explicitly stated in the electrical_isolation_spacing_requirement between the Signal 3 and Power Plane 1. Populate a member of Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure to capture the stated relationships and properties. For further reference in the example, this member will be called 2. Step 4: Create a Material_removal_stratum_feature_template (see figure dim6) for the spacing requirement to be met between Signal 3 and Power Plane 2. This template shape is dependent on the voltage between Signal 3 and Power Plane 2. The distance between the outer extent of Signal 3 and the outer extent of the template shape is explicitly stated in the electrical_isolation_spacing_requirement between the Signal 3 and Power Plane 2. Populate a member of Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure to capture the stated relationships and properties. For further reference in the example, this member will be called 3. As a summary at this point, all the design intent shapes are defined; all the electrical isolation shapes are defined. See figure dim7 for the result achieved by a processor in conformance with this part of ISO 10303, in support of a CAD application, or in support of a manufacturing application. Since Signal 3 has a higher priority than Power Plane 2, the precedent_modification attribute specifies Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure #1 and the subsequent_modification specifies Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure #2

The data associated with this application object are the following:

  • design_intent
  • modification_causal_item

4.2.267.1 design_intent

The design_intent specifies the Stratum_feature that is changed by the Design_intent_modification.

4.2.267.2 modification_causal_item

The modification_causal_item specifies one of either an Assembly_component, a Component_external_reference, a Component_feature, or a Component_feature_external_reference, or one of an Ee_requirement_occurrence, a Stratum, or one of an Inter_stratum_feature, a Laminate_component, a Stratum_feature, or a Stratum_surface that is the cause of the Design_intent_modification.

Formal constraints:

WR1: A processor reading an exchange file based on this part of ISO 10303 shall interpret the lack of member of Design_intent_modification_sequence as meaning that there are no conflicting members of Electrical_isolation_material_removal_structure in the exchange file, and that they may be applied in any sequence.

ENTITY design_intent_modification
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (material_removal_feature,
      material_addition_feature,material_shape_modification))
  SUBTYPE OF (managed_design_object);
    design_intent            : stratum_feature;
    modification_causal_item : restriction_basis_item;
END_ENTITY; -- design_intent_modification
