= Team and Acknowledgments

AP 210 is the product of decades of collaborative work by researchers, engineers, and standards professionals from industry, government, and academia.

== ISO TC 184/SC 4

AP 210 is developed and maintained under *ISO Technical Committee 184, Subcommittee 4* (Industrial data), the same body responsible for the entire STEP (ISO 10303) family of standards.

== Key Contributors

The following individuals have made significant contributions to AP 210 development and its supporting research:

=== Thomas Thurman — AP 210 Project Leader

*Thomas (Tom) Thurman* served as the *AP 210 Project Leader* across all four editions of ISO 10303-210, from the initial Working Drafts through the published International Standards. He is the single most prolific contributor to the AP 210 body of work.

*Standards development:*

* Led the editorial development of all four editions of ISO 10303-210
* Authored the ARM and MIM EXPRESS schemas (38 schema modules bear his CVS commits)
* Directed the AP 210 Rules Model design process, including the formal parameter and recursion structures

*Training and education:*

* Created the complete AP 210 Training CD (July 2003), including the DVD-based course covering AP210 overview, product structure, connectivity, physical design, design rules, and library/design definitions
* Authored the STEP Application Protocols overview presentation (`STEP_APs_Thurman_2000-09.ppt`)
* Delivered the PDES AP210 training program used by industry and government

*Test cases and validation:*

* Authored multiple PDES test cases including the Cylindrical Assembly, Surface Mount Flasher LRU, and Recommended Practices examples
* Created the annotated layer stack region STEP example with detailed comments (2016)
* Co-authored the Wheatstone Bridge test case, the primary teaching example used throughout the AP 210 training

*Industry engagement:*

* Coordinated AP 210 development across PDES Inc., NIST, Boeing, Rockwell Collins, and the ISO TC 184/SC 4 community
* Chaired the AP 210 design rule formalism discussions with Gregory Smith (Boeing) and Manas Bajaj (Georgia Tech)

== NIST

The https://www.nist.gov[National Institute of Standards and Technology^] (NIST) has been instrumental in AP 210's development through:

* Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis for thermal management
* Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GDT) for electronic connectors and packages
* SPICE integration with AP210 mechanical design models
* Reference implementations and validation test cases

== PDES Inc.

https://pdesinc.org[PDES Inc.^] is an industry-government consortium that advances the development and implementation of STEP standards. Their AP 210 contributions include:

* AP210 Viewer development and testing
* IDF-to-AP210 conversion tools
* Training materials and educational resources
* Standards validation and test case development

== The PDES Electrical Project Team

The broader PDES Electrical project team has contributed extensively to the development and validation of AP 210 through test case development, interoperability testing, and implementation feedback.
