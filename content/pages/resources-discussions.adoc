= PDES AP210 Rules Model Discussions

Primary source materials from the PDES AP210 Rules Model design process — the working documents, presentations, and correspondence that shaped how design rules and constraints are represented in AP 210.

== Background

The AP210 Rules Model was developed through a collaborative process involving NIST researchers, PDES Inc. engineers, and industry stakeholders. These discussion materials document the design decisions, trade-offs, and technical rationale behind the rules model architecture.

== Available Materials

`AP210_Rules_Model_Discussion_2003.10.07.ppt`::
Summary presentation of the rules model discussion from October 2003, outlining the proposed architecture and key design decisions.

`Design_rules.ppt`::
Comprehensive presentation on the design rules concept in AP 210 — how manufacturing constraints, electrical rules, and physical requirements are encoded.

`Design_rules_abbreviated.ppt`::
Condensed version of the design rules presentation for executive overview.

`emails_210-rules-model.txt`::
Email correspondence between project participants discussing the rules model approach, entity selection, and scope decisions.

`rule_model_parameter.txt`::
Working notes on parameter representation within the rules model — how electrical and physical parameters are associated with rule constraints.

`rule_query_discussion.txt`::
Discussion of how rules would be queried from AP 210 datasets, including example EXPRESS queries and expected results.

`rules_architecture_development.ppt`::
Presentation on the overall architecture of the rules model, showing how rules relate to the broader AP 210 entity structure.
